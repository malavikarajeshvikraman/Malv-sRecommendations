Friends - The best show

As Monica Gellar once said to Rachel Green: "Welcome to the real world! It sucks – you're going to love it." Could Friends be a more realistic depiction of twentysomething life? From the second Monica ushered Rachel into the world of poverty, dead-end jobs, and quarter-life crises, the series became a beacon for flailing twentysomethings everywhere. And 20 years after its 1994 debut, it still holds up. New Girl or Girls, for example, which cover the same demographic, deliver great jokes and are fantastic shows, but they're not a patch on Friends, which hits on aspects of mid-20s life that have universal appeal.
